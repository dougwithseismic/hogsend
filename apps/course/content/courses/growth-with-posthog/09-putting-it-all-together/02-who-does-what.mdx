---
title: "Who does what"
description: "The division of labour the whole course is built on, in one table — and the three-word rule that keeps the system simple no matter how much you add to it."
---

Every part of this system has exactly one job, and the whole thing stays legible only
because those jobs never blur into each other. You decide what matters: which stages of
the funnel to watch — **AARRR**, the five stages of acquisition, activation, retention,
referral and revenue — and which single **North Star** metric best captures the value your
product delivers. PostHog measures it. Hogsend acts on it. The channels carry the message;
none of the tools ever decides anything.

| Job | Tool |
|---|---|
| Decide what to measure (AARRR, North Star) and own the order | You |
| Collect events, identify people | PostHog |
| See the funnel, retention, leaks | PostHog |
| Run lifecycle journeys (email, Discord, Telegram) | Hogsend |
| Drive paid traffic | Meta ads (or the right channel) |
| Track conversions accurately | Pixel + CAPI (Meta's server-side Conversions API), via PostHog |
| Capture & link identity, referrals | Hogsend connectors |

**PostHog detects. Hogsend acts. You decide.** Hold onto that split and the system stays
simple no matter how much you bolt on — a new channel, a new journey, a new metric each
slot into one of the three roles rather than inventing a fourth. It's a division of labour,
not a pile of tools.
