Everything in this course is a single loop, and the **identity graph** sits at its hub —
the linked record of who each person is across email, Discord, Telegram and the anonymous
session they first arrived in. Every arm of the loop either writes to that graph or reads
back from it:

```
        ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
        │                                                         │
   drive traffic                                                  │
   ads · content · community                                      │
        │                                                         │
        ▼                                                         │
   capture identity                 measure everything            │
   email verify · link Discord  ───► PostHog: funnels, retention  │
   /Telegram · perks for linking     the daily dashboard          │
        │                                  │                      │
        ▼                                  ▼                      │
   ┌─────────── IDENTITY GRAPH ───────────┐                       │
   │ email ↔ Discord ↔ Telegram ↔ anon    │                       │
   └──────────────────┬───────────────────┘                      │
                      │                                           │
                      ▼                                           │
   keep them                                                      │
   lifecycle journeys in Hogsend ──► fire conversions + audiences ┘
   welcome · nudge · win-back ·       back to the ads
   dunning · referral                 → referrals lower CAC
```

Read it as one sentence: **you measure so you can see the leaks; you keep users with
lifecycle journeys — welcome, nudge, win-back, dunning (the automated recovery of failed
payments), referral — so the bucket holds; then you drive traffic and fold every visitor
into an audience you own; that audience feeds better ads and a referral loop, which lowers
your customer-acquisition cost (CAC — what you pay to win one user); and the whole thing
stays measurable because every step fires an event.**

The order runs *measure → keep → grow*, and never the reverse, because of the **leaky
bucket** — a product that loses users about as fast as it wins them. Acquisition is the
last lever precisely because it wastes the most money while the bucket leaks and pays back
the most once it doesn't. Agreeing that order as a team, before anyone spends a pound on an
ad, is the single most valuable decision in the course.
