The course's spine compresses to four gates, and each is a bar you clear before the next
one makes any sense:

1. **Day 0 — commit.** Name the growth owner, write the **activation sentence** — one line
   fixing what "activated" means as a specific event in a specific window, like *"activated
   = a project created within seven days of signup"* — and agree, as a team, that keeping
   users comes before buying them.
2. **Days 1–30 — Measure.** PostHog live, a tracking plan, the **activation event**
   instrumented so that first taste of value actually fires, the daily dashboard built.
   The gate: you can say where the funnel leaks, with numbers, not with a hunch.
3. **Days 31–60 — Keep.** Welcome, win-back, and **dunning** journeys — dunning being the
   automatic chasing of failed subscription payments, the fastest revenue you'll ever
   recover — each run against a **holdout**, a slice of users deliberately left unmessaged
   so you can measure the journey's real lift instead of guessing at it. The gate: lift
   you've measured, not sensed.
4. **Days 61–180 — Grow, then compound.** Open the acquisition tap, capture every new
   visitor's identity into channels you own, and let the whole thing settle into a standing
   experimentation program that keeps improving it.

That's the shape at a glance. The dated version — each phase with its interactive
checklist, exit criteria, realistic targets, and the anti-patterns that blow it up — is a
chapter of its own. This piece is about what keeps the machine alive *after* it's built:
the cadence, and the culture of experiments.
