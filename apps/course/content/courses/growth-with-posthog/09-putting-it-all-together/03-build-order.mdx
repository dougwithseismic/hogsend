The sequence is the course's spine, compressed to four gates:

1. **Day 0 — commit.** Naming the growth owner, writing the activation sentence,
   agreeing retention-before-acquisition as a team (Chapter 0).
2. **Days 1–30 — Measure.** PostHog live, a tracking plan, the activation event, the
   daily dashboard. The gate: you can say where the funnel leaks, with numbers
   (Chapters 1–4).
3. **Days 31–60 — Keep.** Welcome, dunning, win-back journeys — with a holdout. The
   gate: lift you've measured rather than sensed (Chapters 5–6).
4. **Days 61–180 — Grow, then compound.** Open the tap, capture identity into owned
   channels, then let the whole thing become an experimentation program (Chapters 7–8,
   and the experimentation program below).

That's the summary; **Chapter 10 is the full plan** — each phase with its interactive
checklist, exit criteria, realistic targets, and the anti-patterns that blow it up.
This chapter is about what keeps the machine alive *after* it's built: the cadence and
the experimentation culture.
