Every team that builds this system brushes against the same failure modes. It's worth
knowing them by name — each one links back to the chapter with the escape route:

- **Scaling acquisition into a leaky bucket** (Ch 2). The most expensive of the set —
  retention work first is the escape.
- **Chasing someone else's magic number** (Ch 2). The famous activation numbers are
  rally cries, not physics; the correlation-then-experiment method finds *yours*.
- **Building event names dynamically** (Ch 3). One of the quickest ways to ruin an
  analytics setup — one fixed event with variables in properties keeps it queryable.
- **Installing analytics and never looking** (Ch 0, Ch 4). The dashboard graveyard.
  Measurement without a ritual and without action ends up as theatre.
- **Experiment theatre** (the experimentation program). Underpowered tests
  "validating" decisions already made, wins nobody could reproduce, losers never
  written down. Tempo counts learnings, not launches.
- **Trusting the Pixel alone** (Ch 7). It under-counts by a third or more; Pixel *and*
  CAPI, deduped, fed from where you have identity, is the reliable shape.
- **Treating server-side as a consent loophole** (Ch 7). Hashed email is pseudonymous,
  not anonymous — consent still needs capturing.
- **Measuring opens instead of lift** (Ch 5). Opens don't prove causation; holdouts do.
- **Renting your whole audience** (Ch 8). Identity captured into owned channels is
  what saves you from paying to reach the same person twice.
