**Goal: open the tap, and make every drop land in an audience you own.** Only now does
acquisition spend make sense — and for some products, dev tools especially, "the tap" is
content, launches, and community rather than paid ads at all, a case the paid-traffic
chapter makes in full.

**What to take from it:**

- Show up with your *own* plan — if you don't, someone who's never held your role hands you theirs — and let it get looser as you learn, the mirror image of this chapter's gates easing from day-0 checklists to a day-180 rhythm.
- The 30–60 window is where an operator writes two things worth stealing: a first-cut growth strategy and an operating manual for how growth actually works — the same "put the ritual on paper" move as Day 0 here.
- By day 60–90 the job flips from executing to re-planning from evidence, which is exactly what your day-90 gate hands off to the Compound phase.

The checklist below leans on a few ad-tech terms worth having straight before you spend.
The **Conversions API (CAPI)** sends conversions to Meta from your own server rather than
the browser, so ad-blockers and cookie loss don't quietly drop them; you run it alongside
the Meta Pixel and **deduplicate** on a shared event id so one purchase isn't counted
twice. **Event Match Quality (EMQ)** is Meta's 0–10 score for how confidently it can tie
those conversions back to a real person — feed it email and stable identifiers and it
climbs, which the paid-traffic chapter walks through end to end. A **lookalike seed** is
the list of your best users you hand the platform so it can go find more people who
resemble them.

**Exit criteria.** Traffic is flowing from a channel you chose on purpose;
conversions are tracked accurately enough to optimise on (not Pixel-alone guesswork);
and a visible share of new users end up in a channel you own — a verified email, a
linked Discord or Telegram — rather than only in an ad platform's audience you rent.

**The pattern to watch for** is scaling spend before the measurement is trustworthy.
When EMQ is low or events are missing, the ad platform optimises on noise — and the
tuition comes out of your budget. A day spent on conversion quality routinely beats a
week of creative testing.
