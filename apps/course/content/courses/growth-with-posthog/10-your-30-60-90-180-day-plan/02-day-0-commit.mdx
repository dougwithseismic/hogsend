Day 0 is one hour, no code, with the whole team in the room. It exists to settle the two
decisions that quietly sink most growth efforts before they start: nobody actually owns
the loop, and money flows to acquisition before the product can keep anyone. Settle both
out loud, once, and the next 180 days have something to stand on.

<Checklist
  id="plan-day0"
  title="Day 0 — commitments"
  items={[
    "Name the growth owner — one person accountable for measure → keep → grow (a hat, not a hire)",
    "Write the activation sentence: the one user action that means 'got real value', in one sentence",
    "Get the team to agree the order out loud: retention before acquisition",
    "Book the weekly 30-minute growth review, recurring, starting next week",
    "Pick where growth work lives: the same backlog/repo as product work, not a separate doc nobody opens",
  ]}
/>

The last two matter more than they look. A recurring 30-minute meeting is the smallest
possible container for a growth *ritual* — the thing the cadence chapter argues separates
a growth team from a team that ran a growth project once and moved on. And putting growth
work in the same backlog as product work is what makes growth *an engineering discipline*
— a system your team builds and measures, not a budget you hand off — real rather than
aspirational.

Two of those commitments are *writing* commitments — the growth owner and the activation
sentence — so put them down now. Both save to [your workbook](/workbook) alongside
everything else you record in the course, each linked back to the lesson it came from.
