**Goal: see the truth about your funnel.** Nothing in this phase grows the business
directly. All of it determines whether the next five months of work target the real
leak or a guessed one.

Week by week, the shape most teams follow:

| Week | Focus | Chapters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | PostHog live: SDK installed, `identify`/`reset` wired, starter events firing | 1, 3 |
| 2 | Tracking plan written; activation event defined; server-side revenue events | 2, 3 |
| 3 | Reverse proxy; data QA — watch replays, check identities merge correctly | 3 |
| 4 | The daily dashboard built and pinned; alerts to Slack; first honest read | 4 |

**Exit criteria.** You can open the dashboard and say, with numbers: *where* in the
funnel you lose the most people, *what* your week-4 retention roughly is, and *which*
phase of product-market fit you're in (Chapter 4, §4.3). If those three sentences
aren't sayable yet, that's the signal to stay in this phase a little longer —
[YC's analytics-for-startups
guidance](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/8H-analytics-for-startups) is blunt that
most startups' first analytics problem is not volume of charts but trusting what the
few important ones say.

**The pattern to watch for here** is instrumenting forever. Thirty days is enough to
measure a funnel honestly; it is not enough to build a data platform — a schema
registry in week three is usually growth work quietly traded for tooling work.
