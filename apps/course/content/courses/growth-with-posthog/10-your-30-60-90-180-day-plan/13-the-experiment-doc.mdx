The [Compound phase](./06-days-91-180-compound.mdx) asks for a steady tempo of experiments, each with a hypothesis,
a primary metric, and a written result — *including the losers*. The way to make that
routine rather than heroic is a template small enough to fill in before you start and
after you finish. The experiments chapter covers the craft of running experiments well; this is the
one-screen artifact you reuse each time.

The fields that force clarity before you spend the traffic:

- **Hypothesis.** *We believe [change] for [audience] will cause [outcome], because
  [reasoning].* If you can't finish the sentence, the experiment isn't ready.
- **Primary metric + guardrails.** One metric decides it; a couple of guardrails make sure
  you didn't win it by breaking something else. Watching ten metrics means the winner is
  whichever one moved by chance.
- **Minimum detectable effect + how long it runs.** Decided *up front*, so you don't peek
  and stop the moment it looks good — an underpowered "win" is worse than no test
  (the experiments chapter).
- **Result + decision.** Written after: what happened, what you learned, and ship / kill /
  iterate. The losers are the point as much as the winners.

Keep the finished docs somewhere [the weekly review](./12-the-weekly-review.mdx) can see them. A backlog of
scored ideas and a shelf of written results — wins and losses — *is* the experimentation
program; everything else is just remembering to look at it.
