The single most load-bearing commitment in the whole plan is the one that looks smallest:
the recurring 30-minute growth review you booked on Day 0. A plan is a document; a review
is a *ritual*, and the ritual is what the cadence chapter argues separates a growth team
from a team that ran a growth project once and moved on. Meetings that exist in the
calendar get held; intentions that live in a chapter don't.

Keep it to 30 minutes with a fixed agenda, so it survives busy weeks:

<Checklist
  id="plan-weekly-review"
  title="Weekly growth review — the standing agenda"
  items={[
    "North Star + the two or three input metrics beneath it: up, flat, or down since last week?",
    "The one gate we're working toward (day 30/60/90/180) — are we closer?",
    "Experiments: what launched, what's reading, what shipped or got killed (including the losers)",
    "One anomaly worth a look — a replay to watch, an alert that fired, a cohort that dropped",
    "Next week's single most important growth action, and who owns it",
    "Anything blocking the person who owns growth this week",
  ]}
/>

The "anomaly" line is where **cohorts** earn their place — a cohort is a group of users
bucketed by when they signed up, and watching one week's cohort retain (or not) over the
following weeks catches a decay that a topline average would smooth away.

Two disciplines keep it honest. **It's a review, not a status theatre** — the point is to
*decide* (keep going, change course, kill something), not to admire dashboards. And **the
losing experiments get airtime**, because a review where only wins are reported quietly
teaches the team to stop taking risks, and the learning rate is the thing that compounds
(the experiments chapter). A team that can name last week's most useful *failed* test without
embarrassment is running the loop the way it's meant to run.

The most useful thing a review can have is a standing opening question — one sentence the
meeting always starts with, that points everyone at the same goal. "Is the bucket holding?"
early on; "Is growth getting cheaper?" once the flywheel is turning. Pick yours.
