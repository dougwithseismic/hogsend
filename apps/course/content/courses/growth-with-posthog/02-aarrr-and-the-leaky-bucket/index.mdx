**Words you'll meet in this chapter.**

- **AARRR** — the five pirate metrics: acquisition, activation, retention, referral, revenue.
- **CAC** — customer-acquisition cost, what you pay to win one customer.
- **LTV** — lifetime value, the total a customer pays before they leave.
- **LTV:CAC** — the ratio of the two; the single number that says whether growth is profitable.
- **MRR / ARR** — monthly and annual recurring revenue.
- **DAU / MAU** — daily and monthly active users; their ratio is a measure of stickiness.
- **K-factor** — the viral coefficient: how many new users each existing user brings in.
- **Cohort** — a group of users tracked together over time, say everyone who signed up in the same week.
- **NSM** — North Star Metric, the one leading number that best captures the value you deliver.
- **PMF** — product-market fit; the Sean Ellis survey and a flattening retention curve are two reads on it.
- **NPS** — Net Promoter Score, a −100 to +100 gauge of how likely users are to recommend you.
- **UTM** — the campaign tags on a link that mark where a click came from.
- **RARRA** — AARRR reordered to put retention first, as a statement of priorities.
