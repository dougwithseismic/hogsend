> **In this chapter:** the snippet vs npm vs server/mobile SDKs, what autocapture gives
> you free, how to identify users and set properties, the small set of events to track
> first, the tracking plan that keeps a team honest, the other ways to pipe events in
> (Segment, GTM, reverse-ETL), events vs actions, why clean events are the price of
> admission to A/B testing, and the two techniques that beat ad blockers. Code included.

**How this chapter works.** It's broken into ten short pieces — most are a one- to
three-minute read. Where a piece has a video, the idea is the same each time: read the
one-line reason it's worth your time, watch it (or read the transcript underneath), then
read the short digest so the point sticks. Work through them in order, or jump to what you
need:

1. [Ways to install](./01-ways-to-install) — pick the SDK by where the event actually happens.
2. [Autocapture](./02-autocapture) — what you get free the moment `init()` runs, and where it stops.
3. [Custom events](./03-custom-events) — the signal you add yourself, and why money events fire server-side.
4. [Identifying users](./04-identify-users) — connect activity to a person, and the biggest cost lever in the setup.
5. [The events to track first](./05-events-to-track-first) — a small named kit and the tracking plan that keeps a team honest.
6. [Other ways to get events in](./06-other-ways-in) — CDPs, GTM, reverse-ETL, and when each earns its keep.
7. [Beating ad blockers](./07-beat-ad-blockers) — recover the blocked slice, and keep your triggers unblockable.
8. [Actions and experiments](./08-actions-and-experiments) — the retroactive escape hatch, and clean events as test infrastructure.
9. [Keep it clean](./09-keep-it-clean) — the habits that prevent the most common messes.
10. [Before you move on](./10-before-you-move-on) — the afternoon's checklist, then a quiz to check yourself.
