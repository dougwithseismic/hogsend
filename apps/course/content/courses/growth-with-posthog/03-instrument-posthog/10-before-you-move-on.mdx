Six things worth having in place before the next chapter — roughly an afternoon of
work, and everything later builds on them:

1. `posthog-js` installed with `person_profiles: "identified_only"` — the cost default
   that's painful to change later.
2. `posthog.identify()` at login and `posthog.reset()` at logout — the pair that keeps
   identities whole and unmixed.
3. The starter events, with **signup, subscription, and payment firing from your
   backend** — webhook sources of truth, so the numbers stay honest.
4. A **tracking plan**, even if it's ten rows in a Markdown file in the repo — the doc
   that stops two engineers inventing two names for one thing.
5. The managed reverse proxy — a few minutes of DNS that recovers the blocked slice of
   your client-side events.
6. Your one **activation event** firing, QA'd against a session replay — the event the
   whole next chapter's funnel hangs off.

With data flowing, the next chapter is the daily habit that turns all this
instrumentation into decisions.
