---
title: "Beating ad blockers"
description: "10–30% of your client-side events can vanish to ad blockers, and the loss is biased toward technical audiences. Two fixes together: a reverse proxy for the analytics, server-side capture for the triggers that must never be blocked."
---

Ad blockers quietly delete a slice of your data: **10–30% of your client-side events can
simply vanish**, because blockers maintain blocklists of analytics domains and drop any
browser request to `*.posthog.com`. Your charts under-count, and you don't know by how
much. Worse, the loss is *biased* — technical audiences block more, so a dev tool's
charts under-count precisely the users it cares about most.

Two fixes, used together:

### Reverse proxy (recovers client-side events)

Route events through a subdomain of *your own* domain (e.g. `e.yourdomain.com`), which
blockers haven't catalogued. On PostHog Cloud this is a **free, managed** feature:

1. In **Organization settings**, create a managed reverse proxy.
2. Choose a neutral subdomain — *avoid* obvious words like "analytics" or "tracking."
3. Add the CNAME record it gives you; wait a few minutes for SSL.
4. Point your SDK's `api_host` at your subdomain.

This typically recovers 10–30% of otherwise-blocked events. (Note: the managed proxy
runs on Cloudflare and is not compliant with **HIPAA**, the US health-data privacy law —
so don't route **PHI**, protected health information like patient records, through it.
If you self-host the proxy, prefer a Cloudflare Worker over PostHog's managed CNAME,
which can be "uncloaked" by blocklists over time.)

### Server-side capture (immune by design)

A reverse proxy helps the browser. But the truly robust answer for your *important*
events is to fire them from the **backend** with `posthog-node`. Those events go
server → PostHog and never touch the user's browser, so **ad blockers cannot block
them at all.** It's the same reason money-critical events — signup, subscription,
purchase — belong on the backend in the first place.

There's a sharper way to hold this distinction. **The events that drive your lifecycle
journeys are server-side, and therefore ad-blocker-proof.** Your client-side product
*analytics* still take the ~10–30% hit, and only a proxy recovers a slice of it — but
the events that *trigger your emails and Discord messages* fire from your server
regardless of what's running in the browser. Keep the split precise: a proxy mitigates
the *analytics*, server-side capture makes the *triggers* unblockable.
