Two things worth doing while the chapter is fresh:

1. **Fill the one-pager for real.** The template from §13.8 only earns its keep once it
   has your numbers in it — and the blanks you *can't* fill are the most valuable output,
   because they name next month's work precisely.
2. **A look at [your workbook](/workbook).** The board line, the two-number story, the two
   cadence skeletons, the holdout explanation, the honest attribution rewrite, and the
   one-pager draft now sit together. That collection *is* how you'll talk about growth —
   in one place, each entry linked back to the piece that produced it.

The chapter in one line: **decide the altitude before you pick the number, pair every
claim with its mechanism, and say the smaller, defensible thing.** Talking about growth
honestly isn't a soft skill bolted onto the real work — it's the last stage of the same
loop, and the one that decides whether anyone acts on what you found.
