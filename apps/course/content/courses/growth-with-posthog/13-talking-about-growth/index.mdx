**Words you'll meet in this chapter.** The finance shorthand the board and investors talk
in, plus the one measurement idea the whole chapter leans on. Each is also defined inline
the first time it appears:

- **CAC** — customer-acquisition cost: what you spend to win one customer. **CAC payback** is how many months of margin it takes to earn that back; **blended CAC** is the cost averaged across every channel, paid and free.
- **LTV / LTV:CAC** — a customer's lifetime value, and that value set against what they cost to acquire.
- **MRR** — monthly recurring revenue.
- **GMV** — gross merchandise value: the total worth of goods sold through a marketplace (not the same as revenue).
- **ROAS** — return on ad spend: revenue divided by the money spent to earn it.
- **Net revenue retention** — the revenue you keep and expand from existing customers, after churn.
- **Activation rate** — the share of new users who reach first value.
- **North Star metric** — the single metric that best proxies the value users actually get from your product; the number the whole team steers by.
- **Holdout** — a random slice of users deliberately left untouched, so the gap between them and everyone else shows what a program actually added. A **global holdout** does this for a whole lifecycle program at once.
