---
title: "Multi-channel: email is the workhorse, not the only horse"
description: "Different channels do different jobs at different stages — email, in-app, SMS/push/Discord/Telegram — with one trigger branching across channels on unified data, and one shared frequency cap and opt-out."
---

Email carries most of a lifecycle program, but it isn't the only channel — and the
strongest programs pick the channel by the job the moment needs done, because each one
does something the others can't:

- **Email** — highest reach, best for storytelling and education. The workhorse for
  welcome, onboarding, and win-back.
- **In-app** — highest *relevance*, because the user is already in the product. Best
  for activation nudges and, crucially, **referral asks at the aha moment** (the moment
  the product's value has just clicked) — in-product asks convert better than email asks
  because you're catching them while they're holding the value.
- **SMS / push / Discord / Telegram** — urgency and community. Linking Discord and
  Telegram accounts (the own-your-audience chapter) lets community engagement feed the
  *same* journey graph as your emails.

The principle: **one trigger can branch across channels**, on unified data, evaluated in
real time — with frequency capping and cross-channel suppression applied across all of
them. The mistake to avoid is running channels as separate programs with separate
tools and separate opt-out lists: that's how a user who muted your emails gets the
same campaign by push, and how nobody on the team can answer "how many times did we
contact this person this week?"
