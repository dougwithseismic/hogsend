> **In this chapter:** the lifecycle stages mapped to AARRR, the order to build them
> in (and why), why triggered messages beat batch-and-blast, how many touch-points is
> right, how to stay out of the spam folder, the landscape of tools (and when no-code is
> the right call), and how to measure impact honestly with holdouts. This is the
> strategy; Chapter 6 is the code.

**How this chapter works.** It's broken into twelve short pieces — most are a one- to
three-minute read. Four of them carry a short video, with a transcript and a distilled
digest underneath so the point sticks even if you don't watch. Work through them in
order, or jump to what you need:

1. [Lifecycle messaging plugs the bucket](./01-plugs-the-bucket) — the right message, triggered by the right behaviour, at the right time.
2. [The stages, mapped to AARRR](./02-stages-aarrr) — the eight programs and which stage each one serves.
3. [The order to build them — and why](./03-build-order) — welcome first, dunning early if you charge, then win-back.
4. [Triggered beats batch-and-blast](./04-triggered-beats-batch) — how a product event becomes a message, and why the gap is huge.
5. [Why touch-points matter](./05-touch-points) — multi-touch beats single-touch, and the dial you can turn too far.
6. [Deliverability](./06-deliverability) — none of this works if you don't land in the inbox.
7. [Personalise on behaviour](./07-personalise-on-behaviour) — `Hi {{first_name}}` is not personalisation.
8. [Multi-channel](./08-multi-channel) — email is the workhorse, not the only horse.
9. [The tools you could use](./09-the-tools) — the honest map, and when no-code is the right call.
10. [Prove it works: holdouts](./10-holdouts) — opens and clicks don't tell you what a message *caused*.
11. [Which journeys first](./11-which-journeys-first) — how the default order shifts by your kind of startup.
12. [Before you move on](./12-before-you-move-on) — what to settle now, then the quiz.
