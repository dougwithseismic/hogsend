---
title: "Which journeys first, for your kind of startup"
description: "The default build order shifts by company type — self-serve SaaS, e-commerce, consumer app, marketplace, community/web3, and B2B with sales."
---

The default build order — welcome and onboarding first, dunning early if you charge,
then win-back — is a self-serve SaaS default, not a universal law. Your business model
votes on the order too: the widest hole in a commerce funnel sits in a different place
from the widest hole in a marketplace, and the journey that earns its build first is
whichever one plugs *your* biggest leak. Here's how the order reweights by type:

- **Self-serve SaaS** — welcome/onboarding → dunning → win-back, exactly as the default
  has it.
- **E-commerce** — abandoned-cart and browse-abandonment first (the highest-ROI flows in
  commerce), then post-purchase and win-back. Klaviyo territory.
- **Consumer app** — habit/streak and resurrection dominate; onboarding is about
  forming the habit, not "setup."
- **Marketplace** — onboarding has *two* versions (supply and demand), and the key
  retention message is "your side of the market is active" (a new listing, a new buyer).
- **Community / web3** — the link-and-verify and gamification journeys (the
  own-your-audience chapter) *are* the lifecycle program; email supports them.
- **B2B with sales** — onboarding + expansion/upsell matter most; dunning if self-serve
  billing exists; here lifecycle messaging assists the human, it doesn't replace them.

The stages are the same everywhere; only their order changes. Find the widest hole in
your particular bucket, and start there.
