A few things worth settling while this chapter is fresh — each one makes the build in
Chapter 6 noticeably smoother:

1. **Pick the first journey** — welcome + onboarding, for the §5.3 reasons (with
   dunning next if you charge; it's the fastest revenue win in the list).
2. **Map each journey to its events** — the one that triggers it and the one that
   exits it. If the exit event is hard to name, that's usually a sign the journey
   doesn't have a clear goal yet.
3. **Sketch your cadence** per stage and a global frequency cap, so the pile-up
   failure from §5.5 can't happen by accident.
4. **Check the deliverability basics** — SPF/DKIM/DMARC on a sending subdomain and
   one-click unsubscribe (§5.6). Cheap to set up now; expensive to retrofit once a
   domain's reputation is damaged.
5. **Choose your tool deliberately** (§5.9) — and if it's code-first, the next
   chapter is the build.
6. **Plan a holdout** for that first journey, so its lift is measured from day one
   rather than argued about later.

You now know *what* to build and *why*. Next, you build it — in code.
