title: "Feeding it from PostHog (and Hogsend)"
description: "PostHog's Meta Ads destination hashes and forwards conversions server-side with a dedup event_id, builds custom audiences from cohorts without CSVs, and lets Hogsend fire enriched deep-funnel conversions from its own identity graph."
---

You don't have to hand-build a CAPI (Conversions API) integration. PostHog's **Data
Pipelines** include a **Meta Ads conversions destination**:

- You give it your **Pixel/Dataset ID and access token**. PostHog **SHA-256-hashes**
  the email/name, and sends `event_name`, a unique `event_id` (enabling Pixel dedup),
  `event_time`, plus `fbc`/`fbclid` and user agent.
- Use the **same Pixel ID** for the browser Pixel and the PostHog destination so
  deduplication works.
- Filter the destination to **conversion events only** — you don't want every pageview
  going to Meta.

PostHog also auto-captures the ad-click IDs — `fbclid` (Meta's) and `gclid` (Google's
equivalent) — and UTM tags — the `utm_source`/`utm_medium`
parameters on a campaign link — as person properties, and stitches anonymous → identified
when the user gives an email, so a later server-side conversion carries the identifiers
Meta needs for a high EMQ (Event Match Quality — Meta's 0–10 match score).

**Custom audiences without CSVs:** build a PostHog **cohort** (a saved group of people who
share a behaviour, e.g. "closed-won in the last 180 days") and sync it as a Meta custom
audience — for retargeting, or as a lookalike seed. No manual list upload, and it stays
fresh.

**Where Hogsend fits:** Hogsend's events are server-side and keyed to its own identity
graph (email ↔ Discord ↔ Telegram ↔ anonymous). That means Hogsend can fire **enriched,
deep-funnel conversions** — trial→paid, qualified-lead — **server-side, with strong
identity**, which is exactly the high-EMQ, browser-invisible conversion that makes CAPI
worth it. The honest caveat: this makes your *conversions* accurate and your *journey
triggers* ad-blocker-proof, but it does **not** fix the user's client-side PostHog
analytics loss, and it does not override consent or Apple's App Tracking Transparency
(ATT).

### Other ways to send server-side conversions

PostHog's destination is the path this course recommends because you're already in
PostHog, but it's not the only one — the field is worth knowing:

- **Stape / server-side Google Tag Manager** — a popular dedicated server container that
  forwards to Meta, Google, TikTok, etc. More control, more setup.
- **Segment / RudderStack** — if you already run a CDP (customer data platform), it has
  Meta/Google destinations.
- **Direct CAPI integration** — call Meta's endpoint from your backend yourself. Most
  control, most maintenance; only worth it if the managed options can't express what you
  need.

The principle is the same regardless of tool: **send from where you have identity,
dedupe on `event_id`, send only consented conversion events.**
