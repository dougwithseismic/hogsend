**Words you'll meet in this chapter.** This is an acronym-dense chapter — every term is
also defined the moment it first appears, but here's the landing list:

- **CAC** (customer-acquisition cost) — the fully-loaded spend to win one paying customer.
- **LTV** (lifetime value) — the gross-margin a customer contributes over their whole lifetime.
- **Payback period** — how many months of margin it takes to earn back a customer's CAC.
- **MRR** (monthly recurring revenue) — subscription revenue normalised to a monthly figure.
- **ARPA** (average revenue per account) — revenue divided by the number of accounts.
- **CTR** (click-through rate) — the share of people who see something and click it.
- **ROAS** (return on ad spend) — revenue earned for every pound spent on ads.
- **UTM** — the `utm_source`/`utm_medium` tags on a campaign link that record where a visitor came from.
- **Cohort** — a saved group of people who share a behaviour ("signed up last week", "closed-won in 180 days").
- **Lookalike** — an audience Meta builds by finding new people who resemble a seed list of yours.
- **Pixel** — Meta's browser-side tracking snippet, fired from a visitor's browser.
- **CAPI** (Conversions API) — Meta's server-side counterpart to the Pixel, sent straight from your server.
- **EMQ** (Event Match Quality) — Meta's 0–10 score for how well an event's data matches a real Meta user.
- **PII** (personally identifiable information) — data that identifies a person, like an email or phone number.
- **ATT** (App Tracking Transparency) — Apple's iOS prompt that lets users decline cross-app tracking.
- **ITP** (Intelligent Tracking Prevention) — Safari's system for capping and expiring tracking cookies.
- **CDP** (customer data platform) — a tool that collects and routes your customer events (Segment, RudderStack).
