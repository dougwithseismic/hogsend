Two ideas hold this chapter together. First, experiments are a *cost* — traffic, time,
opportunity — so you ship the cheap and reversible changes and save testing for decisions
where being wrong is costly and your intuition might genuinely be mistaken. Second, a test
is only worth running if you can trust the answer, which means: one hypothesis, one primary
metric with guardrails, enough power to see the effect, a control that proves
incrementality, and an honest read that resists the traps. Wrap those in a weekly tempo,
and the learning rate — not any single win — is what compounds.

## Go deeper

- [Ronny Kohavi on Lenny's Podcast — the ultimate guide to A/B testing](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/the-ultimate-guide-to-ab-testing) — trust, invalid results, and when not to test at all, from the person who ran experimentation at Microsoft, Amazon, and Airbnb. ([Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/episode/3vD3c87FT7RKnvZUJRwOb4), [YouTube](https://youtu.be/hEzpiDuYFoE))
- *Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments* (Ronny Kohavi, Diane Tang & Ya Xu) — the standard reference on running A/B tests you can believe: the OEC, guardrails, the peeking problem, Twyman's law, and sample-ratio mismatch.
- [Kohavi — "The Surprising Power of Online Experiments," HBR](https://hbr.org/2017/09/the-surprising-power-of-online-experiments) — the Bing example and the hard evidence that experts can't predict winners.
- [Reforge — the growth experiment management system](https://www.reforge.com/blog/growth-experiment-management-system) — backlog, template, and velocity metrics that turn occasional tests into a program.
- [Lenny's Newsletter — when *not* to run an experiment](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/not-running-an-experiment) — the checklist for shipping without a test.
- [Sean Ellis on high-tempo testing](https://www.saastr.com/sean-ellis-founder-ceo-of-growthhackers-building-a-company-wide-growth-culture-video-transcript/) — why the velocity of tests is the compounding asset.
- [ProductPlan — the ICE scoring model](https://www.productplan.com/glossary/ice-scoring-model) and [Conversion.com — PIE](https://conversion.com/framework/pie-framework/) — two ways to score the backlog; pick one and stop debating.
- [Evan Miller — "How Not to Run an A/B Test"](https://www.evanmiller.org/how-not-to-run-an-ab-test.html) and his [sample-size calculator](https://www.evanmiller.org/ab-testing/sample-size.html) — the peeking problem, and deciding run length up front.
