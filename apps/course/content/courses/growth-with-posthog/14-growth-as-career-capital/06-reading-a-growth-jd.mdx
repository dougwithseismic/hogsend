"Growth" on a job posting covers at least three different jobs, and telling them apart
before you apply saves you from interviewing for a role you didn't want. The same title
can mean a data-driven loop-owner at one company and a cold-email operator at another, so
read the responsibilities, not the header.

**The three shapes of a growth role.** They're three doors into the same loop, weighted
differently:

- **Growth marketer** — leans on the acquisition and top-of-funnel stages: channels,
  campaigns, messaging, landing pages. The best of these still measure and run
  experiments. The weakest just mean "do marketing tasks," with the word *growth* bolted
  on to make the role sound bigger than it is.
- **Growth PM** — owns a slice of the funnel as a product surface: activation,
  onboarding, retention, monetisation. Runs the experiment backlog, works across
  engineering and design, lives in the metrics. Closest to the whole-loop role.
- **Growth engineer** — builds the machinery: instrumentation, experimentation tooling,
  the pipelines and internal surfaces that let the loop run fast. If you came to this
  course as an engineer, this is often the most natural door.

**Green flags** — signs the loop is real:

- A described loop, not a list of channels — the posting talks about measuring, iterating,
  and retention, not just "drive signups."
- Explicit **data access** and instrumentation. If the role owns a metric, you need to be
  able to see it; a posting that mentions the analytics stack is telling you the loop
  exists.
- Experimentation as a norm — A/B tests, holdouts, a backlog. It signals a team that
  knows the difference between activity and lift.
- Growth sits with product and engineering, not stranded in a marketing silo.

**Red flags** — signs it's a channel job wearing a growth costume:

- *Growth = cold outreach only.* If the whole role is outbound volume with no analytics,
  it's a sales-development job relabelled.
- No mention of data, metrics, or measurement anywhere in the responsibilities.
- "Growth hacking" as vibe — a wish-list of tactics with no system, no ownership of a
  number, no instrumentation.
- Everything is acquisition; nothing about activation or retention. That's the leaky-bucket
  team from Chapter 2, hiring someone to pour faster.

Reforge's writing on growth roles and org structure
([reforge.com](https://www.reforge.com)) is the fullest map of how these functions differ
in practice, if you want to go deeper on which door fits you. The short version: match the
posting's *verbs* to the stage of the loop you most want to own, and treat data access as
non-negotiable — a growth role without it is a growth role in name only.
