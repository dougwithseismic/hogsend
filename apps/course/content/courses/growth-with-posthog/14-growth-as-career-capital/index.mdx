> **In this chapter:** what a growth capability is worth now and why full-loop operators
> are scarce, how the workbook you filled in across this course is already a portfolio,
> the difference between "I ran a growth project" and "I run a growth loop" and how to
> tell your story so it reads as the latter, turning your 30/60/90 plan into an interview
> deliverable, the questions you'll be asked (and should ask), decoding a growth job
> description, talking about your impact without over-claiming, and applying the course's
> own owned-audience lesson to your own career. By the end you'll have the artifacts and
> the language to trade what you learned here for a role.

**How this chapter works.** It's broken into nine short pieces — most are a one- to
three-minute read. This is the last chapter of the course, so it's less about new
technique and more about repackaging what you already did into evidence someone will
hire on. Several pieces end with something to write; those save to your workbook next to
the rest of your work. Go in order, or jump to what you need:

1. [What a growth capability is worth](./01-what-its-worth.mdx) — rare skills you can trade for good work.
2. [Your workbook is your portfolio](./02-your-workbook-is-your-portfolio.mdx) — the loop you built is already the evidence.
3. [Project vs. loop](./03-project-vs-loop.mdx) — the one distinction strong interviewers probe for.
4. [The 90-day plan as an artifact](./04-the-90-day-plan-as-artifact.mdx) — turn Chapter 10 into an interview deliverable.
5. [Interviewing for growth](./05-interviewing-for-growth.mdx) — the questions you'll be asked, and the ones to ask.
6. [Reading a growth job description](./06-reading-a-growth-jd.mdx) — green flags, red flags, and what titles really mean.
7. [Attribution humility](./07-attribution-humility.mdx) — why the honest candidate beats the one claiming 10x.
8. [Build your own audience](./08-build-your-own-audience.mdx) — the owned-audience lesson, applied to you.
9. [Before you move on](./09-before-you-move-on.mdx) — a recap, a quiz on the whole chapter, and where to read more.
