Six things worth doing while this chapter is fresh — together they turn the events
you've instrumented into a habit that costs two minutes a day:

1. Create the nine insights from the daily checklist — the fixed list is what makes
   deviations register.
2. Put them on one dashboard, pinned to your homepage, North Star (your single most
   important metric) on top — so the right read is also the path of least resistance.
3. Add alerts on signups, activation, and errors → Slack — few and loud, so a fired
   alert always means something.
4. Schedule a weekly subscription of the dashboard to yourself/your team — alignment
   with zero effort.
5. Read your **retention curve** with the retention piece in hand, and see if you can say
   out loud which phase you're in — fix the bucket, or open the tap. That one sentence is
   the one that decides what you work on next.
6. Watch three session replays of your worst funnel step's drop-outs, arriving from
   the chart rather than browsing cold — the cheapest "why" you'll ever collect.

You can now *see* your funnel and your leaks. The rest of the course is about acting on
them — first by keeping the users you have, then by going to get more.
