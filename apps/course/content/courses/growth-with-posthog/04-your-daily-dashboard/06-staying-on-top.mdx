The pinned dashboard plus alerts is the default. Some alternatives and when they fit:

- **The weekly metrics email/Slack post.** A human (or a subscription) posts the five
  headline numbers with one line of commentary every Monday. Lower-tech than a dashboard,
  higher-signal for a team — the commentary forces interpretation, not just observation.
- **Warehouse BI (Metabase, Looker, Lightdash).** If you've outgrown PostHog's insights
  or need to join heavy financial data, a BI tool on your warehouse is more flexible.
  It's also more setup and another tool to maintain — reach for it when PostHog genuinely
  can't express the question, not before.
- **PostHog's home + "Max" AI.** You can ask Max a plain-language question ("what's our
  D7 retention this month vs last?") instead of building an insight. Good for ad-hoc
  questions that don't deserve a permanent tile.

**What to take from it:**

- Track as little as possible early: copying big-company dashboards at five users just buys
  information paralysis. One metric — week-one retention — comes first, and until it's
  healthy nothing else matters.
- Analytics tells you *what's* broken, not *why* — the retention jump from under 1% to 19%
  came from talking to users (they needed Outlook and Apple Calendar support), not from a
  chart.
- Once retention is decent, per-feature adoption numbers (only 3% create lists, 17% link a
  calendar) become the prioritisation tool — surprisingly low usage usually signals a
  discoverability problem, not a demand one.

The anti-pattern to avoid: **dashboard sprawl.** A team with forty dashboards has none —
nobody knows which is canonical. One pinned board everyone reads beats a wall of charts
nobody trusts.

### For your kind of startup

- **Marketplace** — your daily pulse is *liquidity*: track both sides (supply added,
  demand served) and the match/transaction rate between them. A healthy one side and a
  starved other is the failure mode.
- **Consumer app** — DAU/MAU stickiness and the retention curve are the whole game; paid
  conversion may not even apply.
- **E-commerce** — repeat-purchase rate and time-between-orders replace "retention curve";
  the activation chart becomes first → second purchase.
- **Dev tool** — the activation funnel (docs → first successful call) is your #1 chart;
  signups without a first call are noise.
