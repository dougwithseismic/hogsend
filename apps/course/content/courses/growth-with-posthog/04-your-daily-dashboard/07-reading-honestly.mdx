---
title: "A note on reading numbers honestly"
description: "Three failure modes to avoid as the habit forms: watching vanity metrics, reacting to noise, and reading for confirmation. The fixed daily list is what keeps you honest."
---

Three failure modes to avoid as you build the habit:

- **Watching vanity metrics.** Signups, pageviews, and registered-user counts feel
  good and move easily — [Amplitude's definition](https://amplitude.com/blog/vanity-metrics)
  is blunt: a vanity metric is one that can go up while the business goes nowhere.
  The metrics that reflect *value delivered* — activation, retention, paid
  conversion — are the anchor; the vanity numbers are context around them.
- **Reacting to noise.** Day-to-day numbers bounce. Look at trends over weeks before
  concluding something changed. An alert is for "investigate," not "panic." A single bad
  day is weather; a two-week trend is climate.
- **Reading for confirmation.** The subtle one. If you only dig into charts that look
  wrong when you expected them to look wrong, the dashboard becomes a mirror. The
  discipline is the fixed list read the same way every time (the daily checklist) — it
  forces you past the charts you'd rather skip. This honesty habit is also what Chapter 9's
  experimentation program depends on: a team that reads charts for confirmation will
  run experiments for confirmation too.
