A few things worth doing while this chapter is fresh:

1. **Choose the perk** that's worth linking for (a role, access, credits, a whitelist).
   The sweet spot: valuable enough to convert, not so valuable it attracts farmers — the
   perk-for-linking piece's two design considerations are the test.
2. **Stand up the Discord and/or Telegram connector** with the `/link` email-verify
   flow — this is the moment your list starts becoming a graph.
3. **The `discord-linked` / `telegram-linked` journey** grants the perk and welcomes
   people across both channels; it's the `/link` journey from the Discord piece, nearly
   verbatim.
4. **Give the community a reason to exist beyond the perk**: a mission statement, three
   channels, one weekly ritual — the community piece's whole playbook. The first members
   are worth recruiting by hand — conversation density is what everyone who joins next
   will judge you on.
5. **Place the referral ask at your *aha* milestone** — the first point a user gets real
   value, where the lifecycle chapter fires it — with verified referrals rewarded by a
   role, and it's worth putting "referred-cohort retention" on the dashboard before
   celebrating the invite count.
6. **Sync your verified, consented emails** as a Meta custom audience and lookalike
   seed — this is where this chapter's machine and the driving-traffic chapter's connect.

That's the whole machine. The operating-cadence chapter turns running it into a weekly
rhythm, and the planning chapter sequences everything you've built into a
30/60/90/180-day plan.

## Go deeper

- [Kevin Kelly — 1,000 True Fans](https://kk.org/thetechnium/1000-true-fans/) — the foundational owned-audience thesis: direct reach to a small true-fan base beats large rented reach.
- [Reforge — Growth Loops are the new funnels](https://www.reforge.com/blog/growth-loops) — the loop model this chapter's flywheel implements.
- [Lenny Rachitsky — Building a referrals program](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/this-week-16-building-a-referrals) — mechanics and benchmarks: 15–50% of growth when done right, under 10% of users typically refer.
- [Andrew Chen — Worst users come from referral programs](https://andrewchen.com/worst-users-referral-trials-coupons-gamification/) — the essential counterpoint; read before setting the reward.
- [Chris Dixon — Come for the tool, stay for the network](https://cdixon.org/2015/01/31/come-for-the-tool-stay-for-the-network/) — the strategic ladder from single-player product to network moat.
- [a16z — Hidden Networks](https://a16z.com/hidden-networks-network-effects-that-dont-look-like-network-effects/) — why an identity graph is a network effect that doesn't look like one.
- [Discord — Community resources](https://discord.com/community) — the primary-source playbook for server structure, onboarding, and moderation.
- [CMX — Guide to Building a Community](https://www.cmxhub.com/reports/the-cmx-guide-to-building-a-community) — the fuller community-led-growth framework behind the community piece.
- [Guild.xyz](https://help.guild.xyz/en/articles/6934383-introduction-to-guild) and [Collab.Land](https://docs.collab.land/help-docs/key-features/token-gate-communities/) — the token-gating implementations of the perk-for-linking mechanic.
- [Telegram — deep-link docs](https://core.telegram.org/api/links) — the `?start=` primitive Telegram referrals ride on.
