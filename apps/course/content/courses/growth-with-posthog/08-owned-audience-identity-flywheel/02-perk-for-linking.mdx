title: "The capture mechanic: a perk for linking"
description: "People don't hand over their email or link their Discord out of goodwill — they do it for a perk. The perk is the conversion event, and the conversion is the identity capture. Two rules keep the mechanic honest."
---

People don't hand over their email or link their Discord out of goodwill. They do it for
a **perk.** The pattern is the same across every community that does this well:

> **"Link your account / verify your email → get [a role, a perk, access, credits, a
> whitelist spot]."**

The perk is the conversion event, and the conversion *is* the identity capture. Three
examples worth modelling, from the communities that lean on this hardest:

- **Web3 / token-gating:** Collab.Land and Guild.xyz let a member connect a wallet, the
  bot verifies an on-chain holding, and a role unlocks gated channels or whitelist
  spots. Guild's primitive is literally **Requirements → Roles → Rewards**.
- **Quest platforms** (Zealy, Galxe, Layer3): daily actions earn XP, roles, and airdrop
  eligibility — a structured engagement loop.
- **Email-verify-for-role** (the non-web3 analog, and Hogsend's pattern): the bot links
  to a verification page; the user confirms their **email**; they get a "Verified" role
  and its perks. This is the move that produces an **email ↔ Discord** link you own —
  and several tools market it openly as list-building.

The reward works best when it maps to your product's value and lives *in the owned
channel*, so claiming it deepens the relationship rather than ending it. A perk the
user collects and walks away from buys you one email; a perk they collect *inside*
your community pulls them one step further in.

Two design considerations keep the mechanic honest:

- **The perk must be worth the link, and no more.** Over-reward and you harvest
  perk-hunters who churn the moment the reward lands (the referral-loop piece returns to
  this failure mode under referral fraud). The best perks are things a genuine user
  wanted anyway: access to the community channels, credits in the product, early
  features.
- **Ask at the moment of motivation.** The link prompt converts best right after the
  user got value — the same peak-engagement timing the lifecycle chapter uses for the
  referral ask. A cold DM asking to "verify your email for a role" is spam; the same ask
  as the doorway into a community they just decided to join is onboarding.

Get both right and the perk stops reading as a toll and starts reading as the front
door: a doorway, not a bribe.
