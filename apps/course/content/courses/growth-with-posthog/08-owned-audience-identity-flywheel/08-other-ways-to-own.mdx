Discord/Telegram-on-your-own-infra is this course's emphasis because it builds the graph
you own outright. But "owned audience" is a spectrum, and the alternatives are worth
naming:

- **Newsletter platforms (beehiiv, ConvertKit, Substack).** A hosted email list is a
  genuinely owned audience in the sense that matters most — you can export it and reach it
  without an algorithm. beehiiv even has a built-in referral program. **Use one when**
  email is your whole channel and you don't need the behavioural-journey or identity-graph
  depth. The trade-off: your list and its data live in their platform, and you don't get
  the cross-channel graph.
- **Community platforms (Circle, Slack, Discord, Geneva).** Circle and Slack are
  higher-polish, more B2B-feeling; Discord is the default for consumer/gaming/web3 and the
  one with the richest bot ecosystem for the link-for-perk mechanic. Choose by where your
  audience already is.
- **The fully-hosted CRM/audience suites (HubSpot, Customer.io audiences).** Convenient,
  and they own the data model. Fine if you've decided to rent the infrastructure.

The principle that ranks them: **the more of the identity graph you own outright, the more
the audience compounds and the less you're exposed to a platform's pricing or policy
changes.** A hosted newsletter is a fine, real owned audience; an identity graph on your
own infrastructure is the maximal version. The rung worth picking is the one that
matches how central owned growth is to your business.

### For your kind of startup

- **Consumer / gaming / web3** — Discord is the owned channel; the link-for-a-role
  mechanic is both activation and acquisition. This is your main chapter.
- **Dev tool** — a mix: a high-quality newsletter (changelog/deep-dives) plus a Discord
  or Slack for support and community. The docs-to-Discord path is your loop.
- **B2B SaaS** — email list + maybe a Slack community for customers; referrals are often
  partner/affiliate-shaped (PartnerStack territory) rather than viral.
- **E-commerce** — email + SMS list is the owned audience; post-purchase referral
  (give-get discount) is the canonical loop, and a hosted tool is usually enough.
