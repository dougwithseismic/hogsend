> **In this chapter:** why an owned channel beats a rented ad audience, the
> "link-your-account-for-a-perk" mechanic that captures identity, how email
> verification and Discord/Telegram linking build an identity graph, what actually
> keeps a community alive once you've built one, how the graph makes referral loops
> work (and their honest failure modes), and the off-the-shelf tools you could use
> instead. Code mirrors Hogsend's real connectors.

**How this chapter works.** It's broken into ten short pieces — most are a one- to
three-minute read. Where a piece has a video, the pattern is the same each time: read the
one-line reason it's worth your time, watch it (or read the transcript underneath), then
read the short digest so the point sticks. Work through them in order, or jump to what you
need:

1. [Owned beats rented](./01-owned-beats-rented) — a rented impression evaporates; a captured contact compounds.
2. [The capture mechanic: a perk for linking](./02-perk-for-linking) — the conversion event *is* the identity capture.
3. [Linking Discord, with email verification](./03-linking-discord) — the typed address is never trusted until the email is clicked.
4. [A community worth joining](./04-community-worth-joining) — the linking mechanic captures identity; it doesn't create a community.
5. [Linking Telegram](./05-linking-telegram) — no native email, so the same proof-by-clicking flow binds the edge.
6. [The identity graph](./06-identity-graph) — every link event writes an edge, and the edges compound.
7. [The referral loop](./07-referral-loop) — where the owned graph pays off most, plus its honest failure mode.
8. [Other ways to own an audience](./08-other-ways-to-own) — the spectrum from a hosted newsletter to your own graph.
9. [The full funnel, assembled](./09-full-funnel) — Chapters 7 and 8 joined into one identity-capturing path.
10. [Before you move on](./10-before-you-move-on) — six things to do while it's fresh, then a quiz to check yourself.
