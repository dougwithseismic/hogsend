**Words you'll meet in this chapter**

- **Owned vs rented audience** — a channel you can reach for free forever (a verified email, a linked Discord) versus one you pay to reach every time (an ad audience, a social following).
- **Identity graph** — the web of links between all the ways of reaching one person (email ↔ Discord ↔ Telegram ↔ anonymous web visitor), stitched into a single profile.
- **Custom audience** — a list of your own contacts (usually hashed emails) uploaded to an ad platform so you can target exactly those people.
- **Lookalike seed** — that same contact list handed to an ad platform as an example, from which it finds new strangers who resemble your existing customers.
- **EMQ (event match quality)** — a platform's 1–10 score for how well a conversion you report back matches a real person; verified emails score high, which makes ad optimisation cheaper.
- **CAC (customer-acquisition cost)** — what you pay, on average, to acquire one customer; "blended" CAC averages it across every channel.
- **k-factor** — invites sent per user × the share that convert; above 1 a referral loop is self-sustaining, below 1 it still quietly amplifies every other channel.
- **Cohort** — a group of users bucketed by something they share (signup week, acquisition channel) so you can compare how each group retains.
- **Activation / aha moment** — the first point a new user gets real value (the "aha"), turned into a measurable event like `project_created` within seven days of signup.
- **Last-click attribution** — crediting a signup only to the last link clicked, which loses referrals that span a phone click and a later laptop signup.
- **UTM / fbclid** — tags on a URL (UTM parameters you set, `fbclid` an ad platform adds) that let you trace which campaign a visitor arrived from.
