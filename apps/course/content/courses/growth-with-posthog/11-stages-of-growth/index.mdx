**Words you'll meet in this chapter.** A handful of terms recur across the stages —
here they are in one place, though each is also defined where it first appears:

- **Activation** — the first time a new user reaches the product's value, pinned to a specific action inside a time window.
- **Product-market fit (PMF)** — the point where the market starts pulling the product out of your hands instead of you having to push it.
- **Cohort** — a group of users tracked together because they joined in the same period (a signup week, say).
- **CAC (customer-acquisition cost)** — what it costs in marketing and sales spend to win one paying customer.
- **LTV (lifetime value)** — the total revenue a customer brings before they leave; healthy when it comfortably clears CAC.
- **MRR (monthly recurring revenue)** — the predictable subscription income you book each month.
- **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** — the standard "how likely are you to recommend this?" measure, scored −100 to +100.
- **Holdout** — a group deliberately left out of a change so its true effect can be measured against them.
- **Journey** — an automated sequence of lifecycle messages, like an onboarding email series, triggered by what a user does.
