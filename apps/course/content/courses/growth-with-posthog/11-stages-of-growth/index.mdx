> **In this chapter:** why a growth lever that helps at one stage hurts at another,
> the five stages a product moves through — idea, just-launched, finding
> product-market fit, early traction, and scaling — and for each one the single
> question worth answering, the one metric that matters, the trap most teams fall
> into, and what "good enough to advance" looks like. Then a synthesis of the
> metrics that mislead at every stage. By the end you'll be able to name where you
> actually are, and stop borrowing a later-stage company's answers to questions you
> don't have yet.

**How this chapter works.** It's broken into eight short pieces — most are a one- to
three-minute read. The middle five walk the stages in order; the shape repeats each
time (the question, the metric, the trap, the advance criteria) so the pattern sticks.
Work through them in order, or jump to the stage you're in:

1. [Why stage matters](./01-why-stage-matters.mdx) — the same lever helps here and hurts there.
2. [Idea and pre-launch](./02-idea-and-pre-launch.mdx) — one question, almost no dashboards.
3. [Just launched](./03-just-launched.mdx) — hunt the activation signal, not the signup count.
4. [Finding product-market fit](./04-finding-pmf.mdx) — the flattening curve and the 40% test.
5. [Early traction](./05-early-traction.mdx) — one channel that works before you add a second.
6. [Scaling](./06-scaling.mdx) — the machine, the first hire, and keeping the loop.
7. [Metrics that lie by stage](./07-metrics-that-lie-by-stage.mdx) — which number to distrust when.
8. [Before you move on](./08-before-you-move-on.mdx) — the honest question per stage, then a quiz.
