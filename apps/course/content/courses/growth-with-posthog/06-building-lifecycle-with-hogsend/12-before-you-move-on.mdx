The build order that tends to work, in order of impact, if you want a plan for the week:

1. **Welcome + activation first.** The gap between signup and activation is nearly always
   the biggest leak (Chapter 2), so the welcome and its activation nudge are the highest-return
   emails you can ship. Build these before anything else.
2. **Dunning next, if you charge.** Failed payments are pure, recoverable churn — usually the
   fastest revenue in the whole course for the least work.
3. **Then win-back**, for users who've gone quiet — the same order as Chapter 5, for the same
   reasons.
4. **Put a holdout on that first journey** and read the lift — so when someone asks whether the
   program works, there's a number instead of a feeling.

And hold the *system* in mind from the second journey on: a frequency cap, a priority order, and
one global holdout are what keep a growing pile of emails helpful instead of overwhelming.

You've now plugged the bucket. The rest of the course opens the tap — and makes sure every drop
lands in an audience you own.
