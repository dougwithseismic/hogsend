> **In this chapter:** how an event becomes a running journey, why durable execution
> matters (and what breaks without it), the anatomy of `defineJourney`, email templates
> as code, working examples for each lifecycle stage, the other ways you could build
> this, and the correctness rules that stop you double-sending. Code here mirrors real
> production journeys.

If you'd like to follow along, the examples assume a Hogsend app —
`pnpm dlx create-hogsend@latest` scaffolds one in a minute.

**How this chapter works.** It's broken into twelve short pieces — most are a one- to
three-minute read. One piece (Templates) carries three short videos on building email as
React components: read the one-line reason it's worth your time, watch it (or read the
transcript underneath), then read the digest. Work through them in order, or jump to what
you need:

1. [The loop: event in, journey runs, event out](./01-the-loop) — how an event becomes a durable running journey, and why "durable" is the whole game.
2. [Anatomy of a journey](./02-anatomy-of-a-journey) — `defineJourney`, its meta guardrails, and the context primitives.
3. [Templates: email as code](./03-templates-email-as-code) — React components that render to inbox-proof HTML, versioned with your code.
4. [Welcome + onboarding](./04-welcome-onboarding) — the first journey: welcome now, branch on behaviour two days later.
5. [A behavioural nudge series](./05-behavioural-nudge-series) — sleep, check, send only if needed — the shape most onboarding takes.
6. [Win-back](./06-win-back) — `waitForEvent` gives a dormant user a window to return before the final nudge.
7. [Dunning](./07-dunning) — the highest-ROI journey: recover a failed charge, exit the moment payment clears.
8. [Referral](./08-referral) — ask at peak satisfaction, and only ask users who are still active.
9. [Correctness: exactly-once](./09-exactly-once) — how the engine stops a replay from double-sending, and the one rule you author.
10. [Other ways you could build this](./10-other-ways) — cron, job queues, durable-execution platforms, no-code — and why durable wins here.
11. [Feeding your journeys and proving lift](./11-triggers-and-holdout) — where the events come from, and the holdout that measures your impact.
12. [Before you move on](./12-before-you-move-on) — the build order for the week, then a quiz to check yourself.
