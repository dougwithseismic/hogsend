> **In this chapter:** the lifecycle program made concrete. The lifecycle-strategy
> chapter argued *which* journeys to build and why; this chapter is the playbook for each
> one — what the email
> is, which leak in the funnel it plugs, the trigger, the timing, the content, and how you
> measure it — walked stage by stage across the funnel (Activation → Conversion →
> Reactivation → Revenue → Referral → Feedback), then pulled together into a coordinated
> system. This is strategy and tactics, not code — when you're ready to build it, the
> [Hogsend docs](https://hogsend.com/docs) cover the how.

**Words you'll meet in this chapter.**

- **Journey** — an automated, multi-step message sequence that carries one user through a stretch of their lifecycle: send, wait, check, maybe send again — one person at a time, not a list-wide blast.
- **Trigger** — the single event that starts a journey: a signup, a failed payment, two weeks of silence.
- **Dunning** — the sequence that recovers a *failed payment* (an expired card, a declined charge) before the subscription lapses.
- **Win-back** — a journey that re-engages a user who's gone *dormant*: slipped from active to silent without ever formally cancelling.
- **Holdout** — a small random slice of otherwise-eligible users you deliberately *don't* email, kept as a control so you can measure what the messages actually caused.
- **Cohort** — a group of users defined by a shared trait or moment: everyone who signed up last week, everyone active last month but silent this one.
- **Identity graph** — the map that ties one person's many devices, emails, and sessions to a single identity, so the same human is recognised everywhere.
- **AARRR** — the funnel framework Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue, sometimes called *pirate metrics*.
- **MRR** — monthly recurring revenue: the predictable subscription income each customer adds every month, and loses you when they churn.
