If you're coming from Loops, you've been thinking in three modes: **transactional emails**, **Loops/workflows**, and **campaigns**. Hogsend has a first-class primitive for each — the difference is that the *logic* lives in your repo as code, not behind a builder UI. These recipes are real, copy-pasteable mappings: the Loops call on one side, the exact Hogsend call on the other.

## The three messaging modes

| Mode | What it is | Loops surface | Hogsend primitive |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Transactional** | One-off, system-triggered mail to a single person — verify your email, reset your password, a receipt | Transactional emails (`POST /transactional`) | [`hs.emails.send()`](/docs/recipes/transactional-emails) → `POST /v1/emails` |
| **Lifecycle** | Multi-step, behaviour-driven sequences — onboarding, trial nudges, win-back | Loops / Workflows (the visual builder) | [`defineJourney()`](/docs/recipes/lifecycle-journeys) — durable TypeScript |
| **Marketing** | A one-time broadcast of one template to an audience | Campaigns | [`hs.campaigns.send()`](/docs/recipes/marketing-campaigns) → `POST /v1/campaigns` |

Underneath all three, two more primitives carry the data that drives everything:

- [`hs.contacts.upsert()`](/docs/recipes/events-and-contacts) — the durable contact record (Loops' `updateContact`).
- [`hs.events.send()`](/docs/recipes/events-and-contacts) — the behaviour stream that triggers journeys (Loops' `sendEvent`).

## Which primitive do I reach for?

```text
Sending one email to one person because the system did something?
  → hs.emails.send()           (transactional)        recipes/transactional-emails

Reacting to behaviour over time, with delays and branches?
  → defineJourney()            (lifecycle)            recipes/lifecycle-journeys

Blasting one template to a whole audience, once?
  → hs.campaigns.send()        (marketing)            recipes/marketing-campaigns

Recording who someone is, or what they just did?
  → hs.contacts.upsert() / hs.events.send()           recipes/events-and-contacts
```

## What carries over from Loops 1:1

The shapes deliberately mirror Loops so migration is mostly a search-and-replace:

- `updateContact` → `hs.contacts.upsert({ email, userId, properties })`
- `sendEvent` → `hs.events.send({ name, email, eventProperties })`
- transactional `POST /transactional` → `hs.emails.send({ to, template, props })`
- a Campaign → `hs.campaigns.send({ list, template, props })`
- a Loop / workflow → `defineJourney({ meta, run })` (this is the one that becomes *code* instead of a canvas)

## What's different (and better)

<Callout type="info">
  Three things Hogsend gives you that Loops can't, all of which show up in these recipes:

  - **Typed templates.** `template` and `props` are type-checked against your own React Email registry — Loops' `dataVariables` are untyped strings.
  - **Tracking on transactional mail.** Every send (including one-off transactional) flows through the same tracked mailer, so opens and clicks are recorded *and loop back into the engine as events* that can trigger journeys.
  - **The split.** `contactProperties` (who they are) and `eventProperties` (what happened) are separate bags — see [Events & contacts](/docs/recipes/events-and-contacts).
</Callout>
